Rowing

Rowing builds stamina, strength, and teamwork. As an outdoor weather sport, hardiness and resilience help too!

The movements involved with rowing strengthens the core and back of your body, improving your posture. So if you don't have access to a boat or a club, using a rowing machine at the gym can also bring a lot of health benefits.

On the right is Katherine Grainger, who became a beloved British sporting hero during the London 2012 Olympics when she and her rowing partner Anna Watkins won gold in the double sculls final. She is now the chair of UK Sport.

"When I found the right sport, it opened so many doors and ambitions that I never thought I'd get. Sport has been the biggest enabler for me."
